# Seattle Average Holiday Traffic 2021-2026

## Description
The first chart compares the average daily total of traffic in Seattle between holidays and non-holidays for each weekday from 2021-2026. It is unknown which holidays are counted for this data, and Sunday is the only day without a recorded holiday during this time period. Thursday likely has a high peak due to Thanksgiving traffic but other days do not show a clear pattern. The traffic study counts in the next chart provide more about the lack of data available.

The second visualization displays the total counts of traffic studies in Seattle for each weekday from 2021-2026 and is split for holidays and non-holidays. This matches the previous chart by showing no studies for holidays on Sundays and only 1 for Saturdays. Mondays also have the highest count because most U.S. holidays are observed on Mondays. However, for this five year time period the count is still unusually high compared to the other days. This implies that the records in this dataset are not consistent or structured in the data collection.

The data is sourced from the [City of Seattle Open Data portal](https://data.seattle.gov/) and is provided by SDOT Traffic Counts Group. It contains 445k entries from 2010 to currently April 16, 2026, and was filtered to 2021-2026 for these visuals. It was also filtered for a daily total greater than 5000 to remove errors. The averages and counts are based on holiday status and weekday. The data source does not provide exact locations of data collection and likely does not represent the entire city of Seattle. 2026 is the only year without complete calendar year data but is still included for a current average.
